Linked Open Data

Project overview

La Dolce Vita - Linked Open Data has been developed for the examination of 'Knowledge Organization and Digital Methods in the Cultural Heritage Domain' course of DHDK, University of Bologna, a.y. 2018/2019. For the occasion of the sixtieth anniversary of the release of the movie "La Dolce Vita", we decided to implement a Link Open Data model focused on the production and distribution of this cult movie directed by Federico Fellini in 1960.

What are Linked Open Data?

Data, when isolated, have limited potentiality; on the contrary, their value increases when one or more datasets, produced and published independently and by different subjects, offer the possibility of being integrated and interrogated between them in order to create new shared knowledge.

Advantages:

  • Interoperability- thanks to the use of standard formats, LODs guarantee complete interoperability between different repositories both from a formal point of view (as they are encoded in a common format) and from a conceptual one (as they are defined using homogeneous classes).
  • Reduce information duplication- those who create a dataset can link it directly to existing datasets that they do not have directly.
  • More evidence- Linked Open Data helps generate meaningful links between web pages. This makes it easier for users to discover contents, highlighting the data produced by the institutions and increasing traffic to the websites of the institutions.
  • Authoritativeness - Cultural institutions can establish themselves as authoritative sources of information on cultural heritage, creating a "backbone" for the development of the semantic web.
  • New audience - when users analyse data and use it to create applications such as APIs (Application Programming Interfaces) and mashups, they propose your content to a new audience that would be difficult to reach.
  • Better user experience - by providing users with high-quality and useful information you will improve their user experience; users will therefore be more likely to consult your website.




Workflow

  • Items

    We have made a selection of 10 items of different types coming from archives, museums, libraries websites, and they represent several aspects of the movie "La Dolce Vita".

  • E/R model & Conceptual map

    An entity/relationship model that describes our idea and highlights the relationship between entities.

  • Metadata analysis

    Analysis of the metadata standards used by the various institutions from which we have selected our items.

  • Metadata alignement

    Alignment between the different standards previously selected to describe metadata related to people, places, dates and concepts.

  • Theoretical model

    Theoretical bases on our idea: the theoretical model is mainly based on the E/R model, but it extends it with new properties to properly represent the scenario.

  • Conceptual model

    Creation of conceptual model by reusing existing standard considered the most appropriate ones which allow to represent the terms we need for describing the scenario.

  • Data description

    Description of data on the bases of the previous conceptual model.

  • RDF

    Description of our data in RDF language (Turtle and XML) and graphical representation of them.

  • Connection of data

    Our data connected to other related items through URIs. They are related because of the sharing of one of the features of the object.
